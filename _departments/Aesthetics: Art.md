<p></p>
<h4><strong>Vision:</strong></h4>
<p>
<br>Cultivating Creative and Compassionate Innovators through Artistic Exploration
and Appreciation
<br>
<br>Student Outcomes - Developing the Whole Child</p>
<p>Through the Art Programme, students develop as:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Confident Persons</strong> who express ideas with clarity and pride,</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Adaptable Learners</strong> who explore, innovate, and respond
creatively,</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Respectful Contributors</strong> who collaborate with empathy,
and</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Engaged Citizens</strong> who use art to connect with their community
and culture.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>What We Believe</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Art is a <strong>language for expression, empathy, and understanding</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Artistic learning develops both <strong>creative thinking and character</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Every child can engage meaningfully in the arts.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Curriculum Alignment (MOE Art Syllabus)</strong>
</p>
<p>The Art Department is aligned to the MOE Art Syllabus, with a strong emphasis
on:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Visual Inquiry</strong> – students learn to observe, question,
interpret, and make meaning from visual forms.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Creative Expression</strong> – students generate and develop ideas,
experiment with media and techniques, and communicate personal intent through
art.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Critical and Inventive Thinking</strong> – students evaluate choices,
explore alternatives, and innovate through the art-making process.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Reflection and Communication</strong> – students reflect on their
own and others’ artworks, articulating artistic decisions and learning.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Art Appreciation</strong> – students engage with artworks from <strong>Singapore and the world</strong>,
developing cultural awareness and respect for heritage.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Key Learning Experiences</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Curriculum and cohort-wide programmes</strong> that build strong
foundations in art skills and thinking.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>P4 Museum-based learning</strong> and exposure to local artists
to deepen appreciation and visual literacy.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Master Artist programmes</strong> focusing on developing artistic
talents - for skill refinement and authentic practice.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Competitions, exhibitions, and community projects</strong> to showcase
student voice and creativity.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Talent Development Pathways</strong>
</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Experience:</strong> Foundational exploration and joyful engagement
with the arts.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Enrich:</strong> Skill development, reflection, and guided artistic
growth.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Excel:</strong> Advanced practice, leadership, and contribution
through authentic platforms.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Our Aspiration:<br></strong>We aspire to nurture students who
create thoughtfully, see deeply, and act compassionately. Through the arts,
we seek to develop not only creative skills, but also empathy, reflection,
and purpose. By positioning art as both a creative discipline and a means
of meaningful contribution, we empower students to engage with their school,
community, and the wider world in ways that are thoughtful, responsible,
and impactful.</p>
